About the author

Hello! I’m an environmental scientist with a passion for using data-driven approaches to solve environmental challenges. I specialize in data visualization, geospatial analysis, and workflow automation, with a strong background in both programming (Program R, Javascript) and digital mapping (ArcGIS, QGIS, Leaflet).

Over the years, I’ve coordinated large-scale environmental programs, leveraging my skills in R, JavaScript, and geospatial tools to enhance environmental monitoring efforts. I’m particularly skilled in transforming complex datasets into actionable insights, whether through interactive web maps or automated report-building.

Currently, I work as an Environmental Analyst at the Connecticut Department of Energy and Environmental Protection, where I coordinate CT's three volunteer water monitoring programs: the Riffle Bioassessment by Volunteers (RBV) program, the Volunteer Stream Temperature Monitoring (VSTeM) Network, and Connecticut Lake Watch. My focus is on streamlining data QA, management, analysis, visualization, and mapping processes to improve organizational efficiency and communicate program results with a variety of stakeholders.
